How Britain invented the tank

Tank is any heavily armed and armored combat vehicle that moves on two endless metal chains called tracks. Tanks are weapons platforms that make the weapons mounted in them more effective through their cross-country mobility and the protection they provide for their crews. Weapons mounted in tanks have ranged from single rifle-caliber machine guns to, in recent years, long-barreled guns of 120- or 125-mm (4.72- or 4.92-inch) caliber. This article discusses the development of tanks from the beginning of the 20th century to the present.
